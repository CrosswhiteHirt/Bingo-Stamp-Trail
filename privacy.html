Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Lucky Wave Bingo handles information. The game is a single-player strategy bingo game and does not include user accounts, chat, real-money play, user-to-user trading, or cash prize features.

Information Stored on Your Device

The game may store local preferences such as sound and music settings, plus local gameplay records such as best progress. This information is stored on your device and is used to keep the game experience consistent.

Advertising, ATT, and Identifiers

The iOS project includes advertising SDK dependencies and requests App Tracking Transparency permission before accessing the advertising identifier when available. If you deny tracking permission, the app receives no IDFA from the system. Advertising partners may process device identifiers, ad interaction data, diagnostics, or approximate location according to their own SDK behavior and policies when their services are activated.

The current project includes Chartboost, Yandex Mobile Ads, Vungle Ads, InMobi SDK, and AppMetrica-related dependencies. Ad provider configuration is requested dynamically by bundle identifier, and providers are initialized only when valid configuration is returned.

Security and Diagnostics

The app includes a security framework that may use the app bundle identifier and vendor identifier for integrity or anti-abuse purposes. Standard device diagnostics and crash-related information may be processed by Apple or third-party SDKs as part of normal app and SDK operation.

Children

Lucky Wave Bingo is not designed to collect personal information from children. The game should be used with appropriate parental or guardian supervision where required.

Data Sharing

We do not sell personal information directly. Third-party SDK providers may receive data as described above when their services are active. Their processing is governed by their own privacy policies and platform requirements.

Your Choices

  • You can allow or deny tracking permission in the iOS ATT prompt.
  • You can change tracking permission later in iOS Settings.
  • You can delete the app to remove locally stored game preferences and progress from your device.
